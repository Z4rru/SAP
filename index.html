Key Processes During Sales Order Creation
A
Availability Check (ATP - Available to Promise)
QUESTION: "Can we deliver what the customer wants, when they want it?"
SAP CHECKS:
(+) Current Stock in Warehouse = 100 units
(+) Scheduled Production Orders = 50 units
(+) Scheduled Purchase Orders = 25 units
(‚àí) Already committed to other sales orders = -80 units
(‚àí) Safety Stock (don't touch!) = -20 units
(=) AVAILABLE TO PROMISE (ATP) = 75 units
If customer wants 50: ‚úì Confirmed for requested date
If customer wants 100: ‚ö† Partial delivery or later date proposed
Transaction to check manually: CO09 or MD04
B
Pricing Determination

SAP automatically finds and applies all relevant prices & discounts:

1 Looks at CUSTOMER - Any customer-specific pricing?
2 Looks at MATERIAL - What's the base price?
3 Looks at COMBINATION - Customer+Material special price?
4 Looks at QUANTITY - Any volume discounts?
5 Calculates TAXES based on ship-to location
6 Calculates FREIGHT based on shipping conditions
Access Sequence: Determines the order SAP searches for prices
Step 1: Customer/Material specific price? ‚Üí Not found
Step 2: Price List/Material? ‚Üí Not found
Step 3: Material price? ‚Üí FOUND! $2,495
C
Credit Check
QUESTION: "Can this customer afford this order? Will they pay?"
Customer: Rocky Mountain Bikes
Credit Limit: $500,000
Current Receivables (unpaid invoices): $150,000
Open Orders (not yet billed): $200,000
Credit Exposure: $350,000
Available Credit: $150,000
This Order Value: $124,750
Status: ‚úì APPROVED (within available credit)

If order exceeds credit: BLOCKED for credit review

Credit Manager can release using: VKM1 (blocked orders list)

View/Change credit limits: FD32
